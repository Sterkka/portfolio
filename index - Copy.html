Exploring the Process of Mobile Reporting By Blue-Collar Workers A user research case (2018-)
System

Mobile application and desktop application

Work done

User research, usability evaluation, improvement suggestion, Master's Thesis

In short

As part of my thesis I investigated the use of a mobile reporting solution by construction workers, drivers and maintainers who work for the city of Tampere. The objective was to learn what kind of contextual limitations and usability issues affect the process of reporting data from the field.
How to learn about the utilization of M-Reporting in the field

I carried out a field study in different mobile work settings, interviewed and observed users carrying out real work tasks and interacting with devices. I reported my findings to the partners throughout its duration.

Visits to the field

I took the prototype to a power station, where over-heating of systems can cause severe impacts to the power supply. But first I had to get licensed for Occupational safety (FI: Työturvallisuuskortti) and The Red cross first response (FI: Hätäensiapu).

While testing the feasibility, I used the opportunity to interview an expert about current ways to monitor the systems. After which, it was clear that there aren't many ways and the ways used are very resource heavy.

Benefit to the client

The client was able to learn how their product is used in different contexts. They were also able to pinpoint the positive and the negative aspects of their product. Further, the client benefited from receiving user insight and future process improvement considerations.
