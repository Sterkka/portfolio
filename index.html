Exploring the Process of Mobile Reporting By Blue-Collar Mobile Workers A user research case (2018-2019)
System

Mobile application and desktop application

Work done

User research, usability evaluation, improvement suggestion, Master's Thesis

Synopsis

As part of my thesis I investigated the use of a mobile reporting solution by construction workers, drivers and maintainers who work for the city of Tampere. The objective was to learn what kind of contextual limitations and usability issues affect the process of reporting data from the field.
What was done

I carried out a field study in different mobile work settings, interviewed and observed users carrying out real work tasks and interacting with devices. I reported my findings to my partners throughout the duration of the project.

The project was running in cooperation with Tampereen Infra, which granted access to their workers.

The benefit to the company

The company was able to learn how their product is used in different contexts. They were also able to pinpoint the positive and the negative aspects of their product.

Further, it benefited from receiving user insight and future process improvement considerations.

Mobile work and mobile technology

Mobile workers hold higher levels of accountability to report information about their work tasks nowadays. Their job has distinctive technical characteristics which subsequently affect their ability to answer the demand to use technology in the field.

My visits to the field confirmed that workers may improvise by delaying data entry, by using alternative technologies and by mainly prioritizing their physical work task. This leads to interesting reporting habits.

E-learning Platform For Teachers Service and conceptual design case (2017)
Platform

Website

Work done

User research, interactive prototyping, user testing

Synopsis

The Ministry of Education and Culture's OpenDigi project aims to implement a platform that will help develop the professional competence of teachers. Our task was to conceptualize a digital learning experience and demonstrate it with a prototype.
Great LX starts from understanding the teacher

We've reached out to teachers via forums and social media and they were happy to help us by joining an online group that we've created for discussing and changing ideas.

By hearing what teachers had to say we then were able to draw their needs. We've used other methods such online surveys and phone interviews to gain more understading. At the same time, we've conducted a competitor analysis to assess the strenght and weaknesses of other e-learning platforms on the market.

Gathering was only the first step, next up, ideation

By analyzing and ideating we've learned that the main challenge for the creation of an e-learning platform is supporting individualistic learning paths of teachers from different backgrounds and skill-sets.

After collecting the data, personas were created based on what was learned. During the ideation stage we've envisioned four intriguing concepts to test out with users.

Coneptualizing is part of the deal

Earlier, we drew out the outlines for or four different concepts. Each concept represented a different approach towards e-learning.

The results of our conceptual process were concretized by the creation of wireframes. These were later revamped into slightly higher fidelity mockups that were tested by teachers both online and face-to-face.

Interactive prototype

Subsequently, after we've gathered enough feedback and insight about our initial concpets, the final concept was selected and was ready to be validated. The validation process yielded more ideas for improvements that were finally presented to the client.

Guided By the Light: Light Guided Way Finding System An interaction design case (2017)
System

Custom Rasberry Pi demo + custom UI

Work done

Interaction design, Service design

Synopsis

As part of the HTI university course we were asked to innovate by using modern technology available at our disposal. This led us to explore the restaurant industry and to design an interactive solution that guides restaurant customers to their dining tables.
Researching restaurant usage scenarios

After researching different usage scenarios that occur upon visiting restaurants we've noticed that the procedure of locating a table isn't always so straight-forward for customers. Further, it was discovered that restaurant staff members are often too busy to instruct arriving customers to vacant seats.

Using cheap technology to solve the problem

We found a cheap yet sophisticated way to recognize the amount of entering customers, to direct them to a preferred seat by solely interacting with a touchscreen and to alert the staff upon table occupancy.

Technology used:

  • Custom made prototype UI for user interaction.
  • Microsoft Kinect for recognizing and counting diners.
  • A prototype light guide case built from a Rasberry Pi, LED light and a simple USB camera.
Benefit to the client

The client was able to learn how their product is used in different contexts. They were also able to pinpoint the positive and the negative aspects of their product. Further, the client benefited from receiving user insight and future process improvement considerations.

A Municipal Digital Experience Service Design (2016)
Platform

Mobile app

Work done

User research, benchmarking, prototyping

Synopsis

The municipality of Ylöjärvi wanted us to develop a mobile application concept that will provide citizens a great municipal digital experience. We rose to the challenge and came up with a concept that aims to increase participation, e.g. by voting, reporting hazards and receiving services and information.

A consolidated town that strives for a sense of a community

We headed over to Ylöjärvi to inquire about the local sentiment. After attending a discussion in the town hall we've discovered that local inhabitants would like to take a more active role in social, cultural and political lives, especially since newly consolidated parts of town are located quite far from the center.

The outcome

In our solution we've put our emphasis on bridging geographical gaps and bringing residents together. We've delivered an interactive prototype that was later used to demonstrate to decision makers what could a municipal digital experience feel like.

My part in the project was:

  • Conducting competitor analysis.
  • Creating a gallop survey and analyzing its gathered data.
  • Digital prototyping

University library online database An usability evaluation (2015)
Platform

Website

Work done

Heuristic evaluation, Usability lab evaluation

Synopsis

The universities library had woken up to the fact that their systems were grossly outdated. Their new beta was already in testing and our job was to identify usability issues with the new system.
Everything started with a heuristic evalution

To understand were the potential issues might exist, we started with an expert evalution in the form of a heuristic evaluation. We pinpointed several issues, some major ones and some less severe.

Finally we tested with users

To provide support for our findings and find more issues that might have gone unnoticed, we organized usability testing in a lab. User were given tasks and their progress was recorded and analyzed. Multiple issues were confirmed and potential fixes were communicated to the client.
